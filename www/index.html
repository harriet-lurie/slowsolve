SlowSolve.org

This is a site for puzzles that can be solved in different ways, with varying goals. My general approach is to start with a puzzle game I enjoy, rewrite it, and extend it to suit my own interest.

The online playable versions are older versions of the iPad aps.

Shisen is a mahjong variant that appears to have been popular as an arcade game in Japan in the 1980s. To remove a pair of matching tiles, they must be able to be connected by no more than three vertical and horizontal lines. play_shisen iPad App free App
.
Hexies is a minesweeper game played on a hexagonal grid. play_hexies iPad App free App
.
Trinograms is a triangular version of nonograms, also known as pictograms (although the pictures in this version are randomly generated). The numbers on the stripes next to the grid indicate how many sequential black triangles will be found in that row or diagonal column. play_trinograms iPad App free App
.
Bubble is a bubble shooter without the shooting. Place bubbles to eliminate groups of three or more until all the spaces are empty. play_bubble iPad App free App
.
Match3 is a swap-to-match game with customizable features. Exchange adjacent pieces to eliminate groups of three or more. play_match3 iPad App
.
Merger is a version of 2048 using letters. Click the triangles to slide all letters to a side and introduce a random letter. Like letters combine to form the next letter. play_merger iOS App
.
Hashi is a puzzle where your goal is to connect the circles with the number of paths specified in each circle. Paths must follow the grid, and can not cross each other. When complete, all the circles should be connected in a single network. The maximum number of paths between any two circles can be set in the options - the default is "doubles". play_hashi iPad App
